Help: Link Your Terms to Ontology Vocabulary

In this step, the application has tried to match the terms from your dataset - entities, categories, and relations - to concepts from the ontologies you selected earlier. This process is called ontology based annotation, and it helps connect your knowledge graph to widely-used vocabularies, making it more consistent, searchable, and reusable in the future.

For each of your entity terms, the app suggests a related concept from an ontology. By default, these are linked using a general "has related match" connection, which indicates that your term and the ontology term are associated. This approach keeps your original wording intact while still establishing a meaningful link to recognized, standardized vocabulary.

However, you may find that some suggested matches are exact matches - terms that mean the same thing. In those cases:

  • You can tick the "Replace" checkbox on the right side, to indicate that the ontology term should replace your original entity or category.

For relations (the connecting verbs), we always use the mapped terminology. But here it's important to check whether the direction of the mapped term matches your original meaning:

  • If the direction is reversed, you can tick the "Invert" checkbox to reflect that change.

As with previous steps, your task here is to:

  • Review all suggested annotations
  • Correct any incorrect matches
  • Delete any that don't belong
  • Add new annotations by searching for ontology terms that match your entity, category, or relation

You can add annotations for terms that haven't been matched yet by selecting a label from your entities, categories, or relation verbs, and searching for a matching concept in the ontology database. Once selected, decide whether it's a direct match (tick "Replace") or, in the case of a relation, if it has the opposite direction (tick "Invert").

The goal of this step is to reuse existing vocabulary whenever possible. Instead of redefining terms that already exist in established ontologies, we aim to link to them using their standard identifiers (URIs). This improves the quality and interoperability of your knowledge graph.
