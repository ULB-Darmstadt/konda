Help: Explore and Export Your Knowledge Graph

This final screen lets you explore the knowledge graph that was built from your dataset, documents, and the information you provided in the previous steps. You can use this view to better understand the structure of your data, and to export the graph for future use.

The top section shows an interactive visualization of the knowledge graph:

If an ontology term was marked as a direct match, it may replace the original concept or category entirely in the graph. Relations you defined earlier are also displayed as connections between concepts and categories. It's normal for multiple links to exist between the same nodes - real-world concepts often relate to each other in several different ways.

The terms hasBroader and hasRelated come from a standard (SKOS). They may sound technical, but they help enable smarter queries and future reasoning across graphs.

Below the visual graph:

  • You'll find a text version of the graph, written in a standard format (Turtle).
  • See the ontology behind it - essentially the schema used to organize your data.
  • At the bottom choose a format and export your graph
