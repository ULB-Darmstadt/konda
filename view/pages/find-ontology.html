Help: Add Vocabulary for Your Research

In this step, you'll help the system understand the language and concepts used in your research by selecting vocabularies - called ontologies - that describe your topic.

First, you can enter a prefix and namespace for your graph. These are used in the background when your knowledge graph is created. If you're not sure what to enter here, feel free to leave them blank - the app will use default values.

Next, choose how the app should understand the terms in your research:

  • You can stick with the general-purpose vocabularies (called "base ontologies") that are already included. These cover common and domain agnostic concepts related to research and data.
  • Or, if your research uses more specific terminology, you can add more specialized vocabularies.

To add specific vocabularies:

  • You can upload ontology files if you already have them.
  • Or, you can search for terms that appear in your research using the search box. When you select a term from the results, the entire vocabulary (ontology) that contains that term will be added. This is by design - because that vocabulary likely includes many related terms that can help describe other important aspects of your research, even if they weren't part of your original search.

You can search for as many terms as you like and add multiple vocabularies. You can also remove any you no longer need.
Just keep in mind that the more vocabularies you add - especially large ones - the longer the next step may take, since the app needs to process them for later analysis.
